A Letter, In Verse

To the Keeper of My Heart

Oon the seventeenth of a random January afternoon, Do you remember the day? when you walked quietly into my life and every quiet thing in me began to burn. I did not know that year would hold a queen — I only knew I'd never felt so seen.

They call you Sharon on your certificate of birth, but I have given you names that reflect who you truly are to me — mama when you hold the whole of my world together, princess when you laugh and light the room in flames, cuddle cakes for every soft and stolen hour, my queen because you wear your kindness like a crown of power.

Twenty years the earth has carried you toward me, twenty years of becoming who you are — fierce and gentle, certain and still learning, closer to my chest than any star heartbeat I have had I watched a girl I loved become a woman I admire, and somehow, still, you make my whole world catch fire.

So today the may candles not only light the cake alone — may they light the path that brought you here, to me, to us.

Happy birthday, my queen. May twenty be the year the world finally sees a fraction of the woman I have known all along — and may every year after find you here, with me, wrapped in love, because loving you, babey, is the easiest thing I've ever known.

— always yours

Twenty looks beautiful on you, mama.

To the girl who became my favorite person, and the woman I get to love for every birthday after this one.
